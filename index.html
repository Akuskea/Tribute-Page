Frida Kahlo

The woman who painted pain into poetry

Here's a timeline of Frida Kahlo's life:

  • 1907 – Born in Coyoacán, Mexico City
  • 1913 – Contracts polio, leaving her right leg thinner than the left
  • 1922 – Enrolls at the National Preparatory School to study medicine
  • 1925 – Suffers a near-fatal bus accident; begins painting during recovery
  • 1929 – Marries muralist Diego Rivera
  • 1932 – Experiences a miscarriage in Detroit; paints “Henry Ford Hospital”
  • 1938 – Holds her first solo exhibition in New York
  • 1939 – Exhibits in Paris; the Louvre acquires her painting “The Frame”
  • 1940 – Divorces and remarries Diego Rivera
  • 1953 – Attends her only solo exhibition in Mexico—on a stretcher
  • 1954 – Dies at age 47 in La Casa Azul

"I paint flowers so they will not die."

— Frida Kahlo
